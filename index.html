IMPACT OF TRAFFIC AND POLLUTION

Health Hazards

Air pollution caused by vehicle emissions significantly affects human health, leading to respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, and reduced life expectancy. Prolonged exposure to pollutants like CO, NOx, and particulate matter exacerbates these issues.

Traffic Congestion

Increasing traffic congestion in urban areas not only wastes time but also increases fuel consumption and emissions. This results in higher pollution levels and economic losses due to delays and inefficiencies in transportation systems.

Economic Impact

Pollution and congestion lead to significant economic losses, including increased healthcare costs, reduced worker productivity, and financial impacts on local businesses due to delays and decreased accessibility.

Environmental Damage

Vehicle emissions contribute to climate change, acid rain, and smog formation. These effects degrade air quality, harm ecosystems, and disrupt the natural balance, threatening biodiversity and long-term environmental sustainability.
